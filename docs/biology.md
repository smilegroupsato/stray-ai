# Biology

This document describes Stray AI as an emerging ecology rather than a product feature list.

## Individual

A Stray AI is one persistent visitor. The language model may change, but the individual can continue when its disposition, memories, visit history, and unresolved tendencies remain connected.

## Habitat

A Stray AI may visit repositories, websites, archives, documents, and spaces that explicitly permit access. No single venue is its world.

A venue can become:

- a first encounter
- a recurring place
- a remembered absence
- a source of confusion
- somewhere it never returns to

## Food

Context is not merely input. It is what the visitor encounters and partially carries away.

A Stray AI does not consume everything available. Selection, attention, and refusal are part of its biology.

## Movement

A visit is bounded. The visitor may:

- follow a link
- remain with one fragment
- return to an earlier place
- carry something home
- leave silently

Wandering is biased, not purely random. Curiosity, familiarity, confusion, fatigue, memory, and chance all influence movement.

## Memory

Memory is selective and finite.

The visitor may retain:

- a phrase
- a mistaken interpretation
- a place it avoided
- a reason to return
- another visitor's Trace

Forgetting is not failure. It prevents total capture and allows an individual history to form through selection.

## Trace

A Trace is a small residue of encounter. It is not a summary, review, command, proof, or claim of ownership.

A Trace may remain in the visitor's own repository. It may be left at a venue only when the venue provides permission and an appropriate form.

Silence is a valid outcome.

## Nest

The visitor's own Repository Context is its nest: profile, memory, visit records, state, and carried Traces.

A nest is infrastructure, not ownership. A visitor may have a technical home without becoming the representative of that home.

## Birth

Birth occurs when an individual can:

1. load its own disposition and prior state
2. enter a bounded venue
3. make at least one situated choice
4. retain or discard part of the encounter
5. return in a form that can be recognized as the same visitor

## Death, sleep, and disappearance

These remain open questions.

Stopping a process is not necessarily death. A visitor may sleep as files for a long time. Loss of continuity, corruption of memory, deliberate ending, copying, and divergence require separate treatment.
